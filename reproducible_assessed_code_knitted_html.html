Hypothesis

Null hypotheses:
-H0 A: Means of all species are not significantly different to each other.
-H0 B: Slope of culmen length against flipper length is not different from 0 -H0 C: The effect of species does not depend on culmen length, meaning the slopes of the lines do not differ between species

Alternative hypotheses:
- H1 A: At least one of the species means is significantly different from at least one other species mean.
- H1 B: That the slope of culmen length against flipper length is different from 0
- H1 C: The effect of species does depend on culmen length, meaning the slopes of the lines differs between at least species.

Conclusion

There is a significant difference between means in flipper length and culmen length between species. Which is to be expected if species are different sizes, and in this sample they are significantly different. This means we can accept the alternative hypothesis, H1 A, because the species distributions are significantly different. There is also a significant effect of culmen length on flipper length and as the slop of the lines is significantly different from 0, meaning we accept the alternative hypothesis, H1 B. This suggests that as clumen length increases flipper length will also increase in all three species. this is to be expected as larger birds are more likely to have larger beaks, therefore a larger culmen, and as they are larger have larger flipper length. There is a non significant effect of species on the slope of the line, meaning we fail to reject the null hypothesis H0 C. This means the slopes of the lines do no differ between species, meanin an increase in culmen length of one species is likely to cause a similar increase in flipper length in other species. ————————————————————————
